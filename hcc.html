who we are as people and who we want to be as a nation is all set by the data we are exposed to.
this workshop, sponsored by defindia for the 2025 digital citizen summit, aimed to focus on critical thinking while viewing information and finding new ways to channel data into communication.

half-court collective is a data and design driven collective started by myself and anisha pai, a data scientist and friend.

by half court collective - an iniative by avani samaga and anisha pai

ABOUT ✧˚₊‧

GOAL /

representing data empathetically as opposed to objectively - questioning data and finding personal inferences

using data to broaden the mindset, and encouraging curiosity and critical thinking

how we aim to communicate data, and about the uniqueness of the human experience.

OUTCOME /
  • interactive workshop held for the attendees
  • dialogue with data
  • playing with material, discussing empathy
  • translating data using materials

PROTOTYPE 01
[ women work harder for lesser ]

MESSAGE /

women are given an unfair disadvantage in their work life because of their gender, and are paid less for the same work that their male counterparts do.

to communicate this, we chose to slow scrolling down an informative page, where the person literally must scroll harder to get lesser information out of the page

click here to experience it yourself!

PROTOTYPE 02
[ the word for world is forest ]

MESSAGE /

wildfires are devastating to our natural resources in multiple ways, and despite being the cause of climate change, we have the ability to make a difference.


this project is still a work in progress. the experience is currently incomplete

click here to experience it yourself!

ELEMENTS ✧˚₊‧


CONCEPT

conceptualization was a joint effort, making sure the iterative process was followed. each concept received due diligince of exploration, and we went ahead with ones that highlighted both message and medium adequately.

VISUALS & ANIMATION

all art and animations for the above were hand-drawn for the project, undergoing its own iterative screen based on user interaction.

sound

sound design for 'the word for world is forest' was made using a collection of free-use sounds, coded based on the wildfire stage of the forest - and intended to add life to the forest. for example, a bird call has a higher chance of occuring if a bird has already called out previously.

CODING

entirely coded on vanilla html, css, and js in order for it to be lightweight and easy to access on the phone.

REFERENCES

Charts were adapted from Library Association, Infogram (and the US Census Bureau), NY Times and OurWorldInData. The essay drew on an article on gendered pay-gaps in India in The Hindu Business Line, Kathrin Leuze and Susanne Strauß's paper on why occupations dominated by women pay less, and a video by John Green on this topic.
